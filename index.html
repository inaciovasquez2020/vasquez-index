Mission

Advancing frontier work across the intersections of locality, information, and computation. This program operates as an independent initiative, facilitating high-level theoretical research outside traditional institutional constraints.

The objective is a unified framework for the structure of physical interaction—from Cycle-Rank Rigidity in finite models to the Operational Collapse of wavefunctions.

Current Research Projects

  • Effective Wavefunction Collapse: Resolving the measurement problem via information constraints.
  • Dark Fluid Cosmology: Investigating DFM–MKC frameworks.

Mathematics & Computation

  • EntropyDepth (Chronos): Locality-based P ≠ NP frameworks.
  • Cycle-Rank Rigidity: New invariants in finite-model theory.
  • Navier–Stokes: Algorithmic–energetic rigidity analysis.
